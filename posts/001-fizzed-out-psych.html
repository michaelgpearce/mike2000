The Starfyres – "Captain Dueseldorph" (1967)

Lansford, PA | Burr 1001

If you've never heard this, you're about to experience one of the most gloriously drunk-sounding garage tracks ever committed to vinyl. It sounds like it was recorded inside a submarine that's slowly sinking into a vat of cheap beer. Every instrument is slightly out of tune, the whole thing wobbles and fizzes, and yet every single note hits exactly where it's supposed to.

The Starfyres weren't trying to make a psych masterpiece—they were just a bunch of kids from coal country who happened to stumble into sonic perfection through sheer teenage incompetence. The flip side, "No Room For Your Love," is straight punk fury, but "Captain Dueseldorph" floats in and out of reality like a fever dream.

This is what people mean when they say "shit-fi." Not just bad recording—transcendent bad recording.

The Half Beats – "Should I" (1966)

Chicago, IL | International Recording Co. acetate

Pure venom. This isn't a breakup song; it's a murder confession that just happens to have falsetto backing vocals. The singer doesn't even sound sad—he's sarcastic and fantastically bitter. No saccharine, no bullshit. Just a teenage kid who's done with your lies and wants you to know it.

The Cheater Slicks covered this in 1995 and still couldn't capture the original's biting teenage angst. You can't fake that level of contempt.

Acetate-only, so good luck finding the original. Some dude probably has it in a box in his basement and doesn't even know.

→ YouTube

The Insane – "I Can't Prove It" (1967)

Terryville, CT | Alien Associates 201

The Insane puts this set in yet a higher gear and rush in on the scene with pounding drums and wailing guitars. They just rock out for a full three and a half minutes—lots of soloing and tomfoolery that must've been a hoot to dance to at the local hop.

Connecticut garage at its finest: loud, fast, and utterly unconcerned with whether anyone outside of town would ever hear it.

→ YouTube

The Invictas – "Do It" (1966)

Rochester, NY | Sahara 110

Hard-rockin' soulful pounder with a glorious hook. The Invictas released four fratty 45s between '65 and '67, and this is the one that still hits. Love the ending—they knew exactly when to stop.

→ YouTube

The Lost Ones – "I Can't Believe You" (1966)

Sarver-Butler, PA | Mersey 002

Ultra-crude with a drummer who probably played in the college marching band. You can hear some dude in the background yelling "oh no" and "awww" and "oh yeah" like he's personally invested in this girl's betrayal. Ace guitar solo. The back cover says "I Don't Believe You" but screw it, we're going with what's on the label.

Pennsylvania continues to deliver.

→ YouTube
