No Gigs, All Glory

March 24, 2026

Ten tracks from London SS and the bands that came out of it. The Damned, Chelsea, Generation X, the Boys, the Clash, the Lords of the New Church, Big Audio Dynamite, Sigue Sigue Sputnik, Carbon/Silicon. One rehearsal room in 1975. Zero concerts. Every track has someone who was there.

Comme Si, Comme Ça

March 24, 2026

Ten tracks from the French yé-yé era, 1960–1967. Jacqueline Taïeb, Clothilde, Gillian Hills, Michel Polnareff, Ronnie Bird, France Gall, Jacques Dutronc, Annie Philippe, Marie Laforêt, and Frank Alamo. Bubblegum, beat, and a little weirdness — the case for taking this music seriously.

The Whole Sire Thing

March 18, 2026

Ten tracks from Sire Records, 1976–1979. The Ramones, Flamin’ Groovies, the Undertones, the Rezillos, the Paley Brothers, Tuff Darts, Dead Boys, the Searchers, the Boomtown Rats, and Talking Heads. One label, one ear, a whole lot of bands nobody was signing.

Six Weeks After Budokan

March 17, 2026

Ten tracks from Japan’s Group Sounds era, 1965–1968. The Spiders, the Golden Cups, the Mops, OX, the Tempters, and five more. The Beatles played the Budokan in ’66 and within months every kid in Japan with a guitar was in a band. Most of it never left the country. This is ten of the best.

No Return Address

March 15, 2026

Ten KBD-era punk tracks, 1977–1981. The Pagans, Dow Jones & the Industrials, The Eat, Sado-Nation, Really Red, The Lewd, The Urinals, The Authorities, The Normals, The Dils. Cleveland to New Orleans, small labels and tiny pressings. None of it got through. All of it was right.

Three Minutes, No Witnesses

March 9, 2026

Ten pure power pop tracks, 1976–1991. Flamin' Groovies, the Scruffs, Pezband, the Rubinoos, the Flashcubes, Tommy Keene, and four more across Memphis, Melbourne, Chicago, Zion, and Syracuse. The Big Star lineage, in full.

Ten tracks from the second tier of British glam, 1972–1976. Ricky Wilde at age twelve, Iron Virgin getting buried by McCartney, ex-Sparks musicians going nowhere on Island Records, and seven more that deserved better.

Ten tracks, six countries — UK, France, Germany, Poland, Hungary, Czechoslovakia. The British Invasion sent a signal and Europe answered.

Ten tracks from 1976–1980 that came out fast, sold nothing, and disappeared. Fast Cars, The Nerves, Rubber City Rebels, Pointed Sticks, the Modernettes, and five more. All of them deserved better.
